Apparatus

Materials

  1. Access to a balance
  2. Access to a centrifuge
  3. 1x 250 cm3 conical flask
  4. 2x 100 cm3 beakers
  5. 1x 10 cm3 measuring cylinder
  6. 1x 20 cm3 measuring cylinder
  7. Teat pipettes
  8. Centrifuge tubes
  9. Sample tube and label
  10. Deionised water
  11. 100g Canola oil
  12. 100g Peanut oil
  13. 100g Corn oil
  14. 100g Methanol
  15. 50g 50% Potassium hydroxide solution.

Safety

  1. Need safety goggles in case materials get into eyes
  2. Need fume hood as methanol gas is flamable and poisonous
  3. Need safety gloves as potassium hydroxide is corrosive



How to create biodiesel

  1. First we measured all the materials we needed: 100g of each of the oils, 15g Methanol for each oil, and 1g
    of 50 % Potassium Hydroxide.
  2. placed the beaker containing 100g of oil into the high precision balance.
  3. We added 15 g of methanol into the beaker inside the high precision balance
  4. We added 1g of 50% potassium hydroxide to the solution. Make sure to maintain proper safety precautions
  5. We stirred the solution for 10 minutes
  6. Let it sit and wait for the layers to separate / Centrifuging the solution can also be another option



Testing the effectiveness of biodiesel

  1. We had a setup for the alcohol burner fueled by the biodiesel to boil
    water in the copper cup and the alcohol burner contained 25g of biodiesel
  2. We measured the initial temperature of water in the copper cup
  3. We allowed the fire to burn for 50 seconds
  4. We measured the temperature of the water after 50 seconds
  5. We then compared the results from the three types of oils

Results:

  1. Peanut oil biodiesel | Initial water temperature: 24 | Final temperature: 27
  2. Canola oil biodiesel | Initial water temperature: 24 | Final temperature: 31
  3. Corn oil biodiesel | Initial water temperature: 24 | Final temperature: 29
